The President (spoke in Chinese): The Security
Council will now begin its consideration of the item on
its agenda.

Members of the Council have before them document
S/2016/322, which contains the text of a draft resolution
submitted by the United States of America.

The Council is ready to proceed to the vote on the
draft resolution before it. I shall put the draft resolution
to the vote now.

A vote was taken by Show ofhands.

In favour:
Angola, China, Egypt, France, Japan, Malaysia,
New Zealand, Russian Federation, Senegal, Spain,
Ukraine, United Kingdom of Great Britain and
Northern Ireland, United States of America,
Uruguay and Venezuela (Bolivarian Republic of)

The President (spoke in Chinese): The draft
resolution received 15 votes in favour. The draft
resolution has been adopted unanimously as resolution
2280 (2016).

Following consultations among Council members,
I have been authorized to make the following statement
on their behalf:

"The Security Council recalls its intention to
review progress on steps outlined in its presidential
statement S/PRST/2016/l, of 17 March 2016. The
Security Council welcomes that some progress
was made on the steps outlined in S/PRST/20l6/l,
including progress on implementation of the Juba
security arrangements, including the return of some
members of the Sudan People's Liberation Army
in Opposition to Juba, South Sudan. The Security
Council regrets, nonetheless, that the steps outlined
in S/PRST/2016/l were not fully met and strongly
urges all parties to complete those steps and to
fully implement the 'Agreement on the Resolution
of the Conflict in the Republic of South Sudan'.
The Security Council expresses its intention to
review progress achieved on the steps outlined in
S/PRST/2016/l by 30 April 2016.

"The Security Council reaffirms its unwavering
support for the people of South Sudan."

This statement will be issued as a document of the
Security Council under the symbol S/PRST/2016/3.

The meeting rose at 1.30 pm.
